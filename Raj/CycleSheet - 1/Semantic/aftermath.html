Treaty of Versailles

Signed in 1919, the Treaty of Versailles placed heavy penalties on Germany, including territorial losses, military restrictions, and war reparations.

Long-Term Impact

The war led to the collapse of empires, redrew national borders, and created political instability in Europe. Many historians believe it directly contributed to the outbreak of World War II.

Historical Effects

  • League of Nations
  • Fall of Empires
  • Rise of Extremism
