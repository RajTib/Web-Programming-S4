Overview

World War I (28 July 1914 â€“ 11 November 1918), also known as the Great War, was a global conflict between the Allies and the Central Powers. It involved Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and the Asia-Pacific. The war introduced modern warfare technologies and resulted in millions of military and civilian casualties.

Causes

The war was caused by rising nationalism, imperial rivalries, militarism, and complex alliances. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914 triggered a chain reaction of war declarations across Europe, eventually drawing many nations into the conflict.
